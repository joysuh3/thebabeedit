WHAT MAKES A MARKETING CAMPAIGN ICONIC?

From brand moments that made you cry (in a good way) to pink packaging that made you add to cart- The Babe Edit is your guide to the most empowering, inclusive, and beautifully marketed campaigns in beauty, fashion, and beyond.

Editor's Note

Hi babe,

I'm Joy, and welcome to The Babe Edit, a curated collection of my favorite marketing campaigns that didn’t just sell products, they sold confidence, community, and realness. Think Aerie Real, Dove’s Real Beauty, Barbiecore, and everything in between.

We’re talking about campaigns that feel like warm hugs, glittery dreams, and real-life empowerment.

xoxo,

The Babe Edit

AERIE

A powerful body-positive campaign that ditched retouching and embraced real, unfiltered beauty. Featuring diverse models of all shapes, sizes, and abilities, #AerieREAL encourages confidence and self-love with every wear.

#aeriereal

RARE BEAUTY

This campaign celebrates self-expression in all its forms—glam, soft, bold, or bare. Rare Beauty encourages embracing every version of yourself, with makeup that enhances your natural beauty, not hides it.

#berare

BARBIE

A global marketing phenomenon that painted the world pink. With viral collabs, immersive experiences, and Margot Robbie channeling real-life Barbie looks, the campaign turned a film into a cultural moment.

#pinkmovement

GLOSSIER

This campaign celebrates how beauty products make us feel, not just look. Featuring real people, it highlights the emotional connection to Glossier's products, empowering individuals to feel like their best selves.

#feelinglikeglossier
